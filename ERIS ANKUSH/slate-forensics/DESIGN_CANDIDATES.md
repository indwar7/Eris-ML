## Elimination reasoning

- **Licensing gate** removed #7 (image redistribution unclear), #8 (permission required), #9 (NC), #14, #15 (unlicensed scrapes).
- **Novelty gate (<4/5 or obvious literature match)** removed #3, #4, #5, #11, #12, #16, #17.
- **Infrastructure/determinism gate** (download size, credentialed APIs, hosting rot, >30-min solver runtime) removed #2, #6, #10, #18.
- **Finalists:** #1 (Slate Forensics) vs #13 (ESCI Forensics). #1 wins on: single ~45 MB credential-free static download from a first-party archive with machine-readable license metadata; a real *transactional* substrate that makes the hidden policy genuinely learnable; three heterogeneous targets (audit flag, failure-mode diagnosis, slate repair) with natural hard consistency constraints; and no obvious competition or paper implementing "logged-slate forensics" on this data.

## Selected concept — #1 Slate Forensics

- **Domain:** Recommendation (patterns: *recommendation-policy repair* + *counterfactual recommendation audit*).
- **Genuine substrate:** every transaction, price, timestamp, product code and description that solvers see is the real UCI Online Retail II data, untouched.
- **Synthetic overlay (deterministic, seeded):** a hidden but realistic "You may also like" production policy is replayed at fixed anchor dates against real purchase histories; a seeded minority of emitted slates is corrupted by one of three realistic production failure modes. Labels (flag, mode, healthy slate) exist only because of this overlay — there is nothing to look up, and no original-dataset label is reused.
- **Why it cannot be reduced to a standard Kaggle task:** the primary deliverable is the *reconstruction of the healthy slate* (a ranked structured object over a ~4k-item catalog), which requires imitation-learning the hidden policy from logged examples; flag/mode heads alone (the "classifiable" part) carry a minority of the score, and hard consistency constraints couple all three outputs.
